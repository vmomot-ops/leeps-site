Overview

What the index measures, and why it matters

Ukraine since February 2022 has faced one of the most severe stress tests of a European public‑administration system in the post‑Cold‑War era. The MCRI turns fragmented governance signals into a single, comparable, resilience‑specific score — grounded in resilience‑engineering theory and built only from independently published international data.

Built to be falsifiable

Six design commitments: resilience‑theory grounding, multi‑source data, hybrid objective–subjective weights, a non‑linear interaction term, Shapley attribution, and validation against independent benchmarks.

The headline arc

Steady reform gains (2015–2021), a sharp −34.1% wartime collapse (2022), and a partial +17% rebound by 2024 — still 23% short of the pre‑war peak.

The surprising finding

Digital government was the only dimension to cushion the shock, while the human and rule‑of‑law dimensions drove most of the collapse.

Mathematical Framework

From seven raw signals to one resilience score

Each indicator is aligned so that higher = more resilient, normalised to [0,1], then aggregated with hybrid weights and a single synergy term.

1

Normalisation

Min–max scaling over the full study range maps every heterogeneous indicator onto a common [0,1] axis. Sparse biennial/triennial series (EGDI, SIGMA) are linearly interpolated.

2

Objective weights — PCA

The first principal component explains 68.3% of variance (PC₁+PC₂ = 84.7%), confirming a strong shared "institutional resilience" factor. Weights come from its squared loadings.

3

Subjective weights — AHP

Pairwise comparisons from 12 Ukrainian civil servants, aggregated by geometric mean. Consistency check passes: λmax=7.38, CR = 0.048 < 0.10.

4

Hybrid mixing

A convex blend whose parameter μ is chosen by minimising prediction error against EC Ukraine Progress Reports. Grid search finds μ* = 0.60.

5

MCRI + non‑linear synergy

A linear aggregate plus one multiplicative term capturing the Government‑Effectiveness × Digital‑Government synergy, identified by OLS and clipped to keep it from dominating.

6

Shapley attribution

Cooperative game theory fairly splits each MCRI change across dimensions, satisfying efficiency, symmetry, linearity and the null‑player axiom.

The key insight

Governance resilience under acute conflict is driven mainly by the human, organisational and rule‑of‑law dimensions — Government Effectiveness alone accounts for 38% of the 2022 loss. Yet Digital Government is the lone dimension with a positive Shapley value during the shock: distributed digital infrastructure partially absorbed the blow.

The MCRI and BTI diverge most in 2022–2023, where the MCRI registers a sharper drop — a direct consequence of incorporating the INFORM Lack‑of‑Coping‑Capacity indicator, which reacts immediately to conflict‑induced resource depletion, whereas BTI expert ratings smooth over a longer horizon.

Conclusions

Five findings worth carrying forward

01
A measurable arc. Resilience rose +56% (2015–2021), collapsed −34% in 2022, and recovered only +17% by 2024 — leaving institutions 23% below their pre‑war peak.
02
Trust and integrity dominate. The hybrid weighting puts Anti‑Corruption (25.0%) and Institutional Trust (22.9%) at the centre of resilience, with μ* = 0.60 confirming expert knowledge adds real information.
03
The shock was human, not technical. Government Effectiveness (38%), Rule of Law (29%) and Institutional Trust (18%) drove the 2022 loss — while Digital Government was the only dimension that cushioned it.
04
Integrated reform wins. Only the combined LEEPS‑aligned strategy (S4 ≈ 0.63) restores pre‑war resilience by 2027; single‑dimension strategies all fall short.
05
It validates. Strong convergence with the Bertelsmann Index (r = 0.91) and EC Progress Reports (R² = 0.97) establishes the MCRI as a credible monitoring instrument.

Policy implication

Aid focused mainly on digital or technical capacity risks under‑investing in the human and organisational dimensions that Shapley analysis identifies as the primary resilience drivers — even as distributed digital infrastructure earns recognition as a genuine resilience asset, not merely a service channel.
